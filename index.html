More info

  • The most important weather parameters for a cyclist that can impact performance and comfort during a ride are:

  • Temperature: High temperature can lead to overheating of the body, while low temperature can cause freezing. Proper clothing and adjusting the pace of cycling to the temperature conditions is crucial.

  • Humidity: High humidity can impede the body's ability to dissipate heat through sweating, leading to discomfort and overheating. Low humidity can result in increased evaporation of moisture from the body, leading to dehydration.

  • Wind: Strong winds can affect air resistance and make cycling more challenging, especially when cycling against the wind. It may require more effort, reduce speed, and increase energy consumption.

  • Precipitation: Rain, snow, or other forms of precipitation can affect tire grip, visibility, and overall comfort while cycling. Wet surfaces can be slippery, and rain or snow can affect braking capability.

  • Weather conditions: Occasionally, weather conditions can be extreme, such as storms, gusty winds, or extreme heat. In such cases, safety should be the top priority, and it is advisable to avoid cycling in such conditions.


  • It's important to note that these parameters can have different effects on cyclists depending on their preferences, the weather conditions in their specific location, the type of route, distance, and intensity of the ride. It's essential to adapt to weather conditions and adequately prepare before each training session or race to ensure a safe and efficient cycling experience.
